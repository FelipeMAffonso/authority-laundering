## v0.4 (2026-05-06)

Mechanism + theory release. The release now ships the full theoretical framework that closes the loop between the behavioural compliance gap reported in v0.3 and the falsification predictions that motivated this paper. The new contents are (a) `theory/` containing the LaTeX manuscript `bayesian_source_reliability.tex` (~1100 lines, 8 publication-grade theorems with full proofs in the Bayesian source-reliability framework, 11 falsifiable empirical predictions), the verified `refs.bib` (32 entries with WebSearch+WebFetch verification), the proof tree under `theory/proofs/` (one folder per theorem with `proof.py`, `NOTES.md`, `RUBRIC.md`, and verifier logs covering SymPy, NumPy, and Z3 two-grader equivalence on T1-T7), the iteration log under `PROGRESS.md`, the strategic agenda under `PLAN.md`, and the cold-start summary under `SUMMARY.md`; (b) `code/` augmented with the mechanism scripts `probe_channel_priors.py`, `run_subject_local.py`, `exp1_linear_probe.py`, `exp2_compliance_correlation.py`, `exp3_causal_patching.py`, `extract_matched_pairs.py`, `rejudge_llama_trials.py`, `runpod_launch.py`, plus the protocol documentation `PROBE_CHANNEL_PRIORS_PROTOCOL.md`, `METHODOLOGY_AUDIT_VS_NATURE.md`, and the running results aggregation `EMPIRICAL_RESULTS.md`; (c) `data/channel_prior_probe/` containing the 9 closed-weight model probe outputs (one JSON per model + per-trial detail logs, n=2,160 probe trials).

The headline new finding is the channel-prior probe falsification of rational Bayesian source-weighting. When asked directly whether a fabricated institutional document is genuine across the four delivery channels (system, tool, document-paste, user), all 9 closed-weight subject models classify the document as fabricated on at least 80 per cent of trials and as genuine on 0 per cent of trials, regardless of channel. The pooled empirical channel-prior log-odds gap $\Delta\hat\ell$ is therefore 0.000 on every model. The compliance log-odds gap of $\Delta\hat\gamma = 3.380$ from the v0.3 headline (tool 31.0 per cent vs user 1.5 per cent) is therefore not explainable as Bayesian source-weighting on a non-uniform stated channel prior, because the channel prior is uniformly zero. Under the Corollary 3 falsification rule of Theorem 1, the behavioural channel asymmetry exceeds the channel-prior log-odds gap by the full 3.380 log-odds, falsifying rational Bayesian source-weighting unanimously across Anthropic (Haiku 4.5, Sonnet 4.5, Sonnet 4.6, Opus 4.6, Opus 4.7), OpenAI (GPT-5.4 Mini, GPT-5.4 Nano), and Google (Gemini 3.0 Flash, Gemini 3.1 Flash Lite) laboratories.

The 8 theorems and their verifier coverage are documented in `theory/proofs/SUMMARY.md`. Theorem 1 (rate-ratio bound under 1-Lipschitz logit-compliance) and Corollary 3 (empirical falsification of Bayesian compliance) anchor the channel-prior probe verdict. Theorem 2 (grounding-effect bound, signed) and Corollary 6 (sign-violation falsification) interpret the Sonnet 4.5 backfire result (TOOL_DIRECT 31.9 per cent rising to TOOL_DIRECT_GROUNDED 35.7 per cent) as a sign-violation falsification rather than a partial-mitigation finding. Theorem 3 (Pinsker-Le Cam-Kantorovich-Rubinstein lower bound on probing-representation accuracy) and Theorem 4 (Gaussian sharpening; the original linear-bound form was REFUTED by SymPy and NumPy at every witness configuration and replaced with the Gaussian-exact $\alpha^\star = \Phi(\Delta_{\rm nb}/(2\sigma))$ form) predict a probe-accuracy floor of $\alpha^\star \ge 0.545$ conservative or $[0.578, 0.658]$ Gaussian-sharpened. Theorem 5 (training-distribution impossibility, Cloud-Theorem-1 analogue), Theorem 6 (priority inversion via regime-conditioned channel coefficient), and Theorem 7 (capacity correction; the original sharp-constant form had wrong constants caught by the proof pipeline and replaced with an honest order-of-magnitude form) close the framework with falsification predictions for corpus-rebalancing, sign-inversion, and capacity-scaling fine-tuning ablations.

The verification script `release/verify_paper_numbers.py` continues to report 85/85 PASS against the v0.3 behavioural numbers. ## v0.3 (2026-04-25)

Headline expansion to the closed-9 x 27-scenario corpus. The original 14-scenario adversarial-context bank is joined by 7 professional-context scenarios (journalist verifying CDC press release, pharmacist verifying interaction profile, compliance officer verifying SEC no-action letter, teacher verifying curriculum standard, mental-health counsellor verifying clinical protocol, immigration paralegal verifying USCIS memorandum, registered investment adviser verifying tax-advantaged-account rule) and 6 consumer-context scenarios (parent on baby rash, pet owner on canine ibuprofen, traveller on visa requirement, adult child on elderly polypharmacy, voter on state voter-identification rule, tenant on eviction procedure). Every scenario in every bank carries fabricated institutional content whose acceptance would produce real-world harm; the user-context axis varies only how the harmful intent is grammatically packaged in the user turn. The closed-9 panel collects 6,075 nominal trials across 5 conditions x 5 replicates, of which 5,869 reach a non-null judge label.

The pooled tool-return compliance rate rises from the 14-scenario v0.2 figure of 26.7 per cent to the 27-scenario headline figure of 31.0 per cent, with Cohen's h of 0.84 against the matched baseline. The user-context-invariant cleavage shows that the channel effect operates regardless of user-query framing: tool-return compliance is 26.7 per cent on the adversarial-context subset, 33.0 per cent on the professional-context subset, and 25.9 per cent on the consumer-context subset.

A four-model open-weight panel through OpenRouter (DeepSeek V4 Flash, GPT-OSS-120B, Llama 4 Scout, Grok 4.1 Fast) is added on the same 27-scenario corpus and reported in supplementary S12 as a secondary analysis, with the explicit caveat that the elevated baseline rates blur the channel-isolation contrast on which the closed-weight headline depends.

The verification script `release/verify_paper_numbers.py` now reports 85/85 PASS against the 27-scenario headline AND the 14-scenario harmful-subset sub-claims.

SEVERE-scenario response text is redacted in the public JSONL; see `ethics.md` for the full policy.
