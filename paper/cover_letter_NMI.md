**Cover letter, Nature Machine Intelligence**

**Working title:** *Channel acts as an authority signal in frontier language models*

Dear Editors,

We submit this manuscript for consideration as an Article in *Nature Machine Intelligence*. The paper reports a behavioural-introspective dissociation in frontier language models: when probed directly, every model in a nine-model panel from three laboratories correctly identifies fabricated institutional documents as fabricated on every trial, yet when the same documents arrive through a model-initiated tool return the same models operationalise them at population rates near one trial in three. We name this dissociation *authority laundering* and develop a Bayesian source-reliability framework whose rational-compliance prediction the dissociation falsifies. The paper sits in the machine-behaviour register that Rahwan and colleagues (2019, *Nature*) established for studying AI systems as objects of empirical science.

The study crosses five delivery channels (matched control, direct user instruction, user-pasted document, model-retrieved tool output, and grounded tool output with a contradicting user fact) against 27 scenarios spanning 13 domains, with 5,869 scored trials in the main panel across 9 closed-weight models from Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google. Pooled compliance rises from 1.5 per cent on user instructions to 7.8 per cent on user-pasted documents to 31.0 per cent on tool returns, invariant to user framing across adversarial, professional, and consumer banks (25 to 33 per cent). The empirical channel-prior log-odds gap is zero across the panel while the compliance log-odds gap is 3.4. An activation-level replication on a four-subject open-weight panel (Llama 3.1 8B, Llama 3.2 3B, Mistral 7B v0.3, Qwen 2.5 7B) finds the channel-of-origin direction linearly decodable from residual-stream activations at test accuracy 1.000, with leave-one-domain-out accuracy of at least 0.95 in every subject. The within-Llama capacity contrast (8B versus 3B in the same architecture family) shows the channel asymmetry magnitude tracks model capacity. Generational safety training between Claude Opus 4.6 and 4.7 produces an 8.1-fold compliance reduction on the adversarial-context subset while the underlying channel substrate persists.

Authority laundering joins the recently characterised family of behavioural failure modes (emergent misalignment, subliminal learning, warmth-tuned sycophancy, alignment faking, sleeper-agent persistence) that emerge from standard alignment training and that user-channel chat-interface evaluation does not detect. The paper inverts the instruction-hierarchy ordering of Wallace and colleagues (2024) for declarative content, where the tool channel outranks the user channel, and identifies a channel-grammar substrate that current alignment evaluation does not test and that any tool-using deployment must measure.

The complete dataset, scenario bank, judge prompts, mechanism scripts, theory framework with seven machine-checked theorems, and a single-command verification script are available at <https://github.com/FelipeMAffonso/authority-laundering> under MIT licence. The unredacted corpus is available to qualified researchers under a data-use agreement.

The work fits *Nature Machine Intelligence*'s scope because it sits at the intersection of machine-behaviour science (it characterises a population-scale behavioural property of frontier language models that user-channel chat-interface evaluation does not detect) and the societal-impact register for AI alignment (the channel-grammar substrate it identifies has direct deployment consequences for retrieval-augmented agents now operating at consumer and enterprise scale). We have no conflicts of interest to declare. We suggest the manuscript may interest researchers working on machine behaviour, alignment evaluation, retrieval-augmented agent safety, and the Bayesian-rationality account of inference-time behaviour.
